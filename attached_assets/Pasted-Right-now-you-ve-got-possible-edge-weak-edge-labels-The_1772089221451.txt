Right now you’ve got “possible edge / weak edge” labels. The upgrade is to report:

How much better than random (effect size)

How confident we are it’s not just noise (uncertainty + p-value style evidence)

Is it worth using (a simple “worth it” badge based on thresholds)

Below is a simple, concrete plan that keeps the UI clean.

1) Define “confidence” in a way you can trust
Primary objective (keep it simple)

Since you’re generating 12 lines, use a pack-level metric:

Primary metric:
✅ Δ vs Random (Avg best main matches across the pack)
or whatever you currently use as “Avg Delta vs Random” — just make sure it is consistently defined.

What “confidence” should display (minimum)

For each method (CNF / Optimised / Auto winner), show:

Uplift vs Random: +X% or +X delta

Random baseline spread: random 5th–95th band

Percentile vs Random: e.g. “Better than 78% of random runs”

Permutation p-value (optional but ideal): “Not unusual / Suggestive / Supported”

That’s enough to answer: “Is it statistically worth using?”

2) The simplest “Worth it?” rule

Add a single badge:

✅ Worth trying

If BOTH are true:

Uplift vs random mean is positive

AND the result is outside the random noise band (e.g. above random 95th percentile)

🟡 Promising but not proven

If:

uplift is positive

but inside random band (common with small effects)

⚪ No edge

If:

uplift near zero or negative

This is honest and easy to understand.

3) Where the numbers come from (and why they’re credible)
Random ensemble (already in your app)

You already run 500 random runs in the benchmark. Great.

From those 500, compute:

randomMean

randomStd

randomP05, randomP95

percentile of your method within the random distribution

That gives “how much better than random” in a defensible way.

Permutation test (your “Significance Check”)

This is your “is it likely chance?” check:

show empirical p-value and a badge:

Supported (p ≤ 0.05)

Suggestive (0.05 < p ≤ 0.20)

Unsupported (p > 0.20)

Given what we’ve seen so far, you’ll often be in Suggestive/Unsupported, which is fine — it keeps you from fooling yourself.

5) Make Optimised “confidence” comparable to CNF

Right now, CNF and Optimised “drawFit” are on different scales (as you noticed). Fix that by normalising:

Normalisation rule (simple)

During generation, you already score candidates. Use that distribution to map formulaScore to 0–100:

drawFit = 100 * (score - min) / (max - min)

Then both lanes report drawFit on the same scale.
